# Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

Wicked.jl contributors, maintainers, and community members commit to providing
a welcoming, safe, and professional environment regardless of age, body size,
disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity or expression,
experience level, education, socioeconomic status, nationality, personal
appearance, race, religion, sexual identity or orientation, or technical
preference.

The project values precise technical disagreement. Criticism of an idea,
implementation, benchmark, or design is acceptable when it is specific,
evidence-based, and directed at the work rather than the person.

## Expected behavior

- Communicate respectfully and assume neither expertise nor intent from a
  person's identity or communication style.
- Give actionable technical feedback and accept corrections when evidence
  contradicts a claim.
- Respect differing viewpoints, constraints, and experience levels.
- Keep reviews focused on correctness, maintainability, safety, performance,
  accessibility, and the documented project goals.
- Credit other people's ideas and work.
- Protect private information disclosed through support, security, moderation,
  or review channels.
- Stop behavior when a participant or maintainer identifies it as unwelcome.

## Unacceptable behavior

- Harassment, threats, intimidation, stalking, or sustained disruption.
- Discriminatory jokes, insults, slurs, or demeaning comments.
- Sexualized language, imagery, or attention.
- Personal attacks, deliberate humiliation, or speculation about motives.
- Publishing another person's private information without explicit permission.
- Encouraging others to target, exclude, or harass a participant.
- Repeatedly derailing technical work after a maintainer has set a boundary.
- Retaliation against someone who reports a concern or participates in an
  investigation.

## Scope

This policy applies to project repositories, issues, pull requests, reviews,
discussions, chat spaces, events, and other official project venues. It also
applies when someone represents Wicked.jl in public, including through an
official account, a project email address, or a designated project role.

## Reporting

Do not report a conduct incident in a public issue, pull request, or discussion.
Contact a maintainer privately through a moderation or private-contact channel
listed by the repository host. If no private channel is available, open a public
issue that asks for private maintainer contact without naming participants or
including incident details.

Include, when safe and relevant:

- Where and when the incident occurred.
- A factual description and links or screenshots that preserve context.
- Whether the behavior is continuing or presents an immediate safety concern.
- Witnesses who may be contacted.
- The outcome or protective measure requested.

Reports are handled confidentially to the extent possible. Information is
shared only with people needed to assess the report, protect participants, and
apply a response. Anyone with a conflict of interest must recuse themselves.
False reports made deliberately and in bad faith may themselves violate this
policy; an unsubstantiated good-faith report does not.

Security vulnerabilities are governed by [SECURITY.md](./SECURITY.md), not this
reporting process.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, issues, pull
requests, discussions, and other contributions that violate this policy. A
response should be proportionate to impact, pattern, intent, and risk:

1. **Correction:** a private or public clarification and request to stop.
2. **Warning:** a documented warning with boundaries and consequences.
3. **Temporary restriction:** time-limited removal from project interaction.
4. **Permanent restriction:** indefinite removal from project spaces and roles.

Severe threats, harassment, disclosure of private information, or credible
safety risks may move directly to a temporary or permanent restriction.
Maintainers will prioritize participant safety, preserve relevant evidence, and
avoid public discussion of confidential report details.

## Maintainer responsibilities

Maintainers must apply this policy consistently and in good faith. They are
subject to the same standards as every other participant. A maintainer who
violates the policy, mishandles confidential information, retaliates, or refuses
required recusal may lose moderation or project privileges.

This policy may evolve as the community and its moderation needs develop.
